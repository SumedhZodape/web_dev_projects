Unless NDC works for everyone, it won’t work for anyone. At Travelport, that’s the guiding principle of our NDC approach. Our role in NDC is to shape the change. We’re not spectators — we’re leading participants in this seismic evolution of how travel is bought and sold.

Travelport’s global reach lets us work actively on NDC with the world’s travel agencies and suppliers — not just in one country, continent, or region. When it comes to NDC, one size doesn’t fit all (and hey, who wants to just ‘fit in’ anyway?) — so we’re not building a generic solution.

Last month we launched our next generation platform, Travelport+, the centerpiece of our vision for simpler retailing and multi-source content distribution. Through it, we’re giving our customers tools to reach new retailing objectives like NDC, with simpler, faster, and better access to content. And since our full focus is on the distribution channel, you can trust us to build solutions that benefit both sides of the industry.

Is the Travelport approach to NDC working? Yes. Just take a look at the series of firsts we’ve had (against the backdrop of the pandemic, no less). We were the first retailing platform to secure IATA accreditation for NDC. We’ve secured extended partnerships with leading travel agencies and airlines like American Airlines. And the proof is in the passengers. Like the ones who booked NDC offers through ATPI and Maxim’s Travel using Travelport, and took off with Singapore Airlines and Qantas late last year.

Q1 2021 Leadership Council Meeting

But let’s get back to the NDC Leadership Council. We created this steering group in 2019 to facilitate industry-wide dialogue and collaboration on NDC, and to address potential challenges along the way. Travelport chairs the meetings and periodically publishes the outcomes for the benefit the entire industry.

The latest (virtual) meeting of the Travelport-hosted NDC Leadership Council took place in Q1 2021. In attendance was a group of over 20 of the world’s most influential travel companies from around the globe — including airlines, travel agencies, corporate travel managers, and corporate booking tool providers.

To encourage open, honest dialogue, council meetings are run under Chatham House Rules. This means that while we may publish findings, we don’t mention participant views by name.

Headlining the Q1 meeting agenda was a poll to gauge which NDC servicing capabilities are most important. Understanding this is key to successfully achieving NDC adoption at scale. The Council asked members to rank both the servicing capabilities they consider as ‘blockers’ (i.e., obstacles to NDC adoption), and also the servicing capabilities they consider to be most important. Ranking can be a point of contention, because most Council members find all of these areas important, but some are higher priority. But overall, it provides great insight, and is a valuable, tried-and-tested practice that keeps NDC on track to deliver benefits for the whole industry, and for customers.
